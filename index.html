Unified analytics platform & stakeholder governance

Creating a single source of truth for product, marketing, sales & customer success

Role: Product Analyst
Company: Tungsten Automation
Domain: SaaS analytics platform
Stakeholder leadership Data governance KPI design

Context & problem

Product, Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success each had their own reports and metrics, leading to conflicting views of adoption, funnel performance, and customer health. Leaders lacked a single, trusted view of "how the product is really doing" and which levers to pull.

  • Complex data landscape across product usage (PostgreSQL), GA, Salesforce, Marketo, Zendesk, and BigQuery.
  • No standardized KPIs or intake process for new analytics needs.
  • Ad hoc reporting consumed analyst time and delayed decisions.

What I did

  • Led a cross-functional data governance initiative, identifying 15+ key stakeholders and establishing weekly/bi-weekly syncs by function.
  • Designed a Jira-based requirements intake form capturing business question, desired outputs, data sources, timelines, and success criteria.
  • Partnered with stakeholders to define core product, revenue, and engagement KPIs and documented them in Confluence as the single reference.
  • Mapped and integrated data from product logs, GA, Salesforce, Marketo, Zendesk, and BigQuery into a harmonized model powering 20+ Tableau / Data Studio dashboards.
  • Created presentation templates for executive reviews: context, methodology, key insights, and prioritized recommendations.

Impact

  • Shortened product decision cycles by 35% by aligning on standard KPIs and always-available dashboards.
  • Saved 85 hours/week of manual reporting effort, worth roughly $220K per year.
  • Enabled data-backed roadmap and campaign decisions that contributed to $1.8M ARR in additional revenue and $450K in churn prevention.
  • Increased stakeholder satisfaction with data insights from 67% to 90%, based on quarterly surveys.
Single source of truth across 6+ systems 20+ live dashboards Executive & functional views

Onboarding simplification & experimentation program

Using A/B testing to redesign trial onboarding and improve conversion & retention

Role: Product Analyst / Experimentation Lead
Domain: SaaS onboarding funnel
Scope: Trial → paid conversion, feature adoption, support volume
Hypothesis-driven discovery Experiment design Funnel analytics

Context & problem

New users were dropping out of a 5-step onboarding flow before they experienced value. Session recordings, support tickets, and funnel data showed that 42% abandoned at integration setup, long before they processed a single document.

  • Goal: improve trial → paid conversion and reduce onboarding-related support tickets.
  • Constraints: preserve necessary configuration for enterprise customers while simplifying for SMBs.

What I did

  • Conducted qualitative and quantitative discovery: GA/Amplitude funnels, Hotjar session recordings, user interviews, and 200+ onboarding-related support tickets.
  • Formulated a test hypothesis: reducing steps and automating integrations would increase conversion by 15% and lower abandonment at integration setup.
  • Designed a multi-variant A/B test (control 5-step vs two 3-step variants), calculated required sample size and test duration, and partnered with UX and engineering on flows.
  • Defined tracking events and instrumentation plan across Optimizely, Segment, and product analytics to measure activation, feature adoption, and retention by segment.

Impact

  • Increased trial → paid conversion by 19.8% relative lift on the winning variant, driving approximately $1.2M in incremental ARR.
  • Reduced onboarding-related support tickets by 42% and improved 90-day retention by 22% among users who went through the simplified flow.
  • Improved time-to-first-value by 38%, enabling more users to activate within the trial window.
  • Scaled the experimentation program from 1–2 tests per quarter to ~15 per quarter, with a knowledge base of 20+ documented experiments informing future roadmap decisions.

Invoice processing launch & anomaly monitoring

Using dashboards as an early-warning system during a high-stakes feature release

Role: Product Analyst
Domain: AI-powered invoice processing feature
Scope: Launch monitoring, incident detection, remediation
Release monitoring Root-cause analysis Cross-functional response

Context & problem

The team was launching a flagship AI invoice processing feature after months of engineering work. We needed to monitor adoption, accuracy, and conversion, and be able to react quickly if anything went wrong in production.

  • Instrumented tracking for key events: setup completion, first successful invoice, errors, and funnel drop-offs.
  • Built an hourly-refresh Tableau dashboard as "mission control" for PMs, engineers, and leadership.

What I did

  • Defined launch success metrics with the Product Manager: adoption, processing accuracy, time savings, and impact on trial → paid conversion.
  • On launch, monitored KPIs daily; when conversion suddenly dropped, sliced data by segment and use case to isolate the issue.
  • Identified a spike in file-upload errors (2% → 18%) tied to large invoice PDFs after a code change. Quantified the impact in affected users and estimated ARR at risk.
  • Partnered with engineering and support to prioritize the fix, then tracked recovery and shared daily updates with stakeholders until metrics normalized.

Impact

  • Prevented an extended revenue impact by catching and helping resolve the bug within ~24 hours instead of weeks.
  • Turned the incident into a company-wide case study on data-driven release monitoring and incident response.
  • Strengthened trust in analytics as part of the release process, leading PMs to treat dashboards as a required launch artifact.
Real-time anomaly detection Daily stakeholder updates Faster incident resolution
